ICVS, who we are

The Life and Health Sciences Research Institute (ICVS) was established in 2003 to develop biomedical research within the School of Medicine of the University of Minho.

Currently, the ICVS is a multidisciplinary unit that brings together scientists, engineers and clinicians in a unique scientific and technological environment encased within over 6000¬†m2, benefiting from research expertise in different fields such as bioimaging, cell and molecular biology, deep learning and computer science, electrophysiology, epidemiology and statistics, structural and functional MRI analysis, medical electronics, microbiology with ABSL2/3 facilities, regenerative medicine, motor and cognitive animal behavior, oncobiology and surgical endoscopy. Our knowledge encompasses the complete R&D pipeline: from in vitro research to animal models, drug discovery and pre-clinical validation, to clinical trials, and finally to reach the market as innovative medical solutions.

Over the last decade, the ICVS has developed synergies with several partners with complementary expertise, namely the 3B‚Äôs Research Group ensuing the establishment of the PT Government ICVS/3B‚Äôs- Associate Laboratory, local healthcare providers and the Clinical Academic Centre ‚Äì Braga Association (2CA-Braga), devoted to performing clinical research. The ICVS is also integrated several national research infrastructures, among which PT-OPEN and EATRIS.PT, for drug discovery and development.

The ICVS develops projects in national and international consortia, providing experience and training to young researchers, welcomes foreign students and researchers, and launches initiatives to foster international institutional collaborations, such as those established with the International Iberian Nanotechnology Laboratory (INL), the Centro de Investigaci√≥ns Biom√©dicas (CINBIO) and the Radboud University.

Along with its several Spin-offs, the ICVS aims to contribute as a key stakeholder for the upholding of a competitive Cluster in Biomedical Science, Technology and Healthcare, steered by the School of Medicine, including the 2CA-Braga, the Digital Medicine center P5, and the knowledge transfer structure B.ACIS. Aligned with this vision, the ICVS aspires to boost its complete research pipeline to create scientific knowledge and value, producing Research Outputs that improve Clinical Outcomes.

Research Integrity and Ethics

Introduction

Research integrity is a cornerstone of scientific excellence and social trust. At ICVS, we align our practices with the Code of Ethical Conduct of the University of Minho, the European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity (ALLEA), and frameworks such as ones promoted by the University of Minho on Harassment and Discrimination and the UNESCO Recommendation on Science and Scientific Researchers. This guide aims to orient students to uphold these principles throughout their academic journey.

Fundamental Principles

Research integrity at ICVS is grounded in a commitment to the following core principles:

Honesty: being truthful at every stage of the research process, from data collection to the communication of results.

Accountability: taking full responsibility for the research process, from conception to publication.

Rigor: applying the highest standards in the design, execution, analysis, and reporting of research.

Transparency: clearly disclosing methods, data sources, and any conflicts of interest.

Fairness: ensuring equitable recognition in collaboration, authorship, and peer review.

Respect: upholding the dignity, welfare, and rights of all involved in research, including humans, animals, and ecosystems.

These principles form the foundation of responsible science and are expected of all ICVS researchers, regardless of their career stage.

Institutional and Legal Frameworks for Research Ethics and Integrity

National and University-Level Structures and Regulations

Academic Regulations of the University of Minho (RAUM): Provides general information on the organization and functioning of the different cycles of studies/courses offered by the University of Minho, rights and responsibilities of the University of Minho academic community, and on academic procedures.

Code of Ethical Conduct of the University of Minho: Sets out the core ethical principles and standards expected from all members of the University of Minho academic community. It addresses areas such as professional integrity, conflicts of interest, and responsible behaviour in academic and institutional settings. The Code also outlines preventive measures against corruption and defines mechanisms for accountability and sanctions in cases of misconduct.

Ethics Council of the University of Minho: Advises on ethical matters and promotes reflection on ethical issues pertinent to the University of Minho academic and research community.

Ethics Committees of the University of Minho: Committees with specific responsibility for providing informed assessments on the procedural and ethical compliance of research and development (R&D) activities. These include scientific, technological, or experimental projects conducted across the various disciplinary fields and within the academic and research units of the University of Minho.

ORBEA  MAGDA

National Ethics Committee for Clinical Research (CEIC): An independent public ethics body operating under the Ministry of Health. While not a regulatory authority itself, its legally binding ethical opinions are required for the authorization of clinical trials and certain biomedical studies in Portugal. It plays a central role in the national framework for ethical oversight of human research. It issues the single ethical opinion required for clinical trials, evaluates study protocols in terms of risk-benefit, consent, investigator qualifications, and participant safety, and coordinates the national ethics committee network. CEIC also promotes ethical literacy, public trust in clinical research, and alignment with international standards.

National Council of Ethics for the Life Sciences (CNECV): An independent advisory body reporting to the Portuguese Parliament. It issues ethical opinions and recommendations on matters related to life sciences, biomedical research, emerging technologies, and health policies. The CNECV plays a key role in promoting national dialogue and reflection on complex ethical issues in science and healthcare.

Lei n.¬∫ 12/2005: Establishes the legal framework for the collection, use, and protection of personal genetic and health data in Portugal, with particular relevance for biomedical and clinical research.

Decreto-Lei n.¬∫ 113/2013: Establishes the rules governing the protection of animals used for scientific purposes in Portugal, in line with EU Directive 2010/63/EU. It regulates the ethical review, authorization, housing, care, and use of animals in research, as well as training requirements and institutional responsibilities.

Decreto-Lei n.¬∫ 21/2014: Establishes the legal framework for clinical research in Portugal, including the requirements for ethical approval, informed consent, and trial registration.

Decreto-Lei n.¬∫ 80/2018: Regulates the organization, operation, and responsibilities of ethics committees in healthcare institutions, universities, and biomedical research centers conducting clinical studies.

Lei n.¬∫ 58/2019: Adopts and enforces the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the Portuguese legal system, governing the processing of personal data, including in scientific research contexts.

International Frameworks

European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity (ALLEA): Provides a comprehensive framework for good research practices across all scientific disciplines and research settings. It defines principles such as reliability, honesty, respect, and accountability, and offers guidance for dealing with misconduct, data management, authorship, and collaborative research.

UNESCO Recommendation on Science and Scientific Researchers: A global standard-setting instrument adopted by all UNESCO Member States, reinforcing the responsibilities of researchers and institutions. It addresses issues such as freedom of research, ethical oversight, societal responsibility, and the role of science in sustainable development.

Singapore Statement on Research Integrity: Sets out 14 universal principles for responsible conduct in research, including honesty, accountability, and good stewardship. Widely cited in codes of conduct and by funding agencies worldwide.

OECD Integrity and security in the global research ecosystem: Offers policy-level recommendations for promoting a culture of integrity, strengthening institutional frameworks, and managing research misconduct.

Declaration of Helsinki (World Medical Association): A cornerstone document for medical and health research ethics involving human participants. It outlines key principles such as informed consent, risk-benefit assessment, protection of vulnerable groups, and independent ethical review, and serves as a global benchmark for researchers and ethics committees.

Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion
